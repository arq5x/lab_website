BIMS 6000

2012-Present

For the past two years, I have been co-instructing the Analyzing DNA and Genome Variation section of the UVA BIMS core first year course with Ira Hall. This section of the core curriculum addresses modern DNA sequencing techologies and their applications as well as an overview of human genetic variation. We also cover a detailed "hands-on" session where students are able to explore real genome sequencing data and genetic inheritance patterns in a mother, father, and child "trio".

{ External Teaching }


CSHL Adv. Sequencing

2008-Present

I have been involved with the Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory's Advanced Sequencing Technologies Course since 2007: first as a student, then as a Teaching Assistant, and since 2009, I have been an instructor with Elaine Mardis, Richard McCombie, my Ph.D. mentor Gabor Marth, and Mike Zody. This is an intensive course that covers a wide range of applications of DNA sequencing to the study of genome biology. The course covers both "wet lab" and "dry lab" techniques by introducing students to both the molecular biology and computational techniques required to apply DNA sequencing to genomics research. In recent years we have had exceptional students from around the world and we combine lecture and hands-one exercises from the instructors with guest lectures from genomics experts.

Each year, we maintain a Wiki page housing the lectures, exercises and techniques that are covered in the course. If you are new to this area of research or are curious about current techniques, have a look at the 2011 Course Wiki. In particular, the lectures and exercises may be informative.


Canadian Bioinformatics Workshops

2013-Present

In the summer of 2013, I, along with Michael Stromberg, Marc Fiume, and Francis Ouellette had the pleasure of teaching in the Canadian Bioinformatics Workshop's course on Informatics for High Throughput Sequencing Data. My role is to discuss the molecular and informatics techniques required for identifying and characterizing rearrangements in genome structure (a.k.a. "structural variation").
